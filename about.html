About Me

From 2015-2023, I studied equivariant algebraic geometry and Gromov-Witten theory under Ionut Ciocan-Fontanine at the University of Minnesota. I wrote my thesis on confluence theorem for small K-theoretic I-function for Calabi-Yau GIT targets and then provided application to the mirror map from string theory. From 2011-2015, I studied Math and Physics at Swarthmore College.

These days when not working on computer programming projects or math education, you can find me hiking, running, watching basketball, playing piano, or playing PC games.

I am lover of hard problems and elegant solutions.
